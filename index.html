About Hyperspace Graph Paper

Hyperspace Graph Paper (HSGP) is a visualisation technique used for navigating through Boolean hyperspaces, and for viewing structure in the state space of Boolean systems.

HSGP uses a dimensional unfolding trick to provide a two-dimensional projection of a high-dimensional Boolean space. In short, odd dimensions are unfolded horizontally and even dimensions are unfolded vertically. If you draw a square and label the corners (0,0), (0,1), (1,0), (1,1) going from top-left to bottom-right, it's like that… except that you keep going. For a more comprehensive explanation, try out the demo above, or read some of the references.
